      <p class="label">The Research Program</p>

      <h1>The Maha Framework</h1>

      <p class="question">One question, pursued across every scale at which it appears:&nbsp; what governs the organization of complex adaptive systems?</p>

      <p class="lede">Physics asks what governs matter. Biology asks what governs life. This body of work asks what governs <em>organization itself</em> — the recurring grammar by which complex systems, from civilizations to cells to minds, hold their form against decay.</p>

      <p class="lede dim">What follows is not a catalogue of products. It is a map of an inquiry in progress: where it began, where it has reached, and where it is going next.</p>

    </div>

  </section>

          <p class="dir">Civilization → Inward</p>

          <h3>The Maha Principle</h3>

          <p class="work">The Architecture of Human Flourishing</p>

          <p>Begins with the visible crises of modern life — metabolic, attentional, relational — and works inward to the coupled failure beneath them. How complex human systems flourish, stagnate, and renew.</p>

        </div>

        <div class="axis">

          <p class="dir">Cognition → Outward</p>

          <h3>The Orbital Mind</h3>

          <p class="work">The Astrophysics of the Self</p>

          <p>Begins with the oldest stable system we know intimately — the solar system — and works outward to the mind, using orbital organization as a descriptive map for cognition. How a single intelligence generates and coordinates systems in the first place.</p>

        <p class="label">The Work</p>

        <h2>An Evolving Body of Work</h2>

        <p>The framework expresses itself in several forms — a planned six-volume sequence of books that argue it, research that tests it, and tools that apply it. The arc moves outward in scale, from the individual to the species to the cosmos. One book is nearing release; the rest are in development or planned, and are marked as such. What follows is a map of the inquiry, not a catalogue of finished products.</p>

      </div>

            <h3><a href="https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.20746123" rel="noopener">The M·A·H·A Framework</a></h3>

            <p class="kind">Working paper · An Integrative Architecture for Resisting Systemic Metabolic, Attentional, and Relational Extraction</p>

            <p>The formal foundation. Formalizes the framework as an integrative socio-biological model: that contemporary metabolic, attentional, and relational pathologies are coupled downstream consequences of a single extractive architecture. Develops the proposed couplings, sets boundary conditions, and concludes with a formal falsifiability protocol — every claim tagged by evidence level.</p>
