The Research Program

The Maha Framework

One question, pursued across every scale at which it appears:  what governs the organization of complex adaptive systems?

Physics asks what governs matter. Biology asks what governs life. This body of work asks what governs organization itself — the recurring grammar by which complex systems, from civilizations to cells to minds, hold their form against decay.

What follows is not a catalogue of products. It is a map of an inquiry in progress: where it began, where it has reached, and where it is going next.

The Spine · Two Directions, One Question

Civilization → Inward

The Maha Principle

The Architecture of Human Flourishing

Begins with the visible crises of modern life — metabolic, attentional, relational — and works inward to the coupled failure beneath them. How complex human systems flourish, stagnate, and renew.

Cognition → Outward

The Orbital Mind

The Astrophysics of the Self

Begins with the oldest stable system we know intimately — the solar system — and works outward to the mind, using orbital organization as a descriptive map for cognition. How a single intelligence generates and coordinates systems in the first place.

The Work

An Evolving Body of Work

The framework expresses itself in several forms — a planned six-volume sequence of books that argue it, research that tests it, and tools that apply it. The arc moves outward in scale, from the individual to the species to the cosmos. One book is nearing release; the rest are in development or planned, and are marked as such. What follows is a map of the inquiry, not a catalogue of finished products.

The Books · A Six-Volume Arc

Phase I · The Individual

Securing and understanding the single human system

The first three books work at the scale of one person — the body, the mind, and the relationship between a human and the machines now augmenting it.

I · The Maha Principle

Nonfiction · The Architecture of Human Flourishing

The foundation. Argues that metabolic decline, attentional capture, and social disconnection are one coupled failure, and lays out the M.A.H.A. framework for rebuilding the physical and cognitive baseline.

Releases Jul 10 2026 · Pre-order

II · The Orbital Mind

Nonfiction · The Astrophysics of the Self

Maps the organization of the solar system onto the architecture of cognition — a descriptive framework, grounded in dissipative-systems theory and neuroscience, for navigating one's own psychology.

In development

III · The Synthetic Self

Nonfiction · On Artificial Intelligence

Examines machine intelligence as a compression of the human record — and argues that the alignment problem is, at root, a human problem reflected back at us. How to integrate AI as a genuine partner rather than a dependency.

Planned

Phase II · The Species

From individual to lineage

With the individual system in view, the inquiry scales up to ask how the questions of enhancement, intention, and design play out at the level of humanity as a whole.

IV · The Omega Point

Nonfiction · On Intentional Human Futures

A book-length examination of the questions raised as humanity gains tools to shape its own biology and trajectory — what it means to move from undirected change toward deliberate, ethically fraught design. Explored as open inquiry, not prophecy.

Planned · Conceptual

Phase III · The Cosmos

From the biological to the philosophical

The final two volumes are openly speculative philosophy — extending the framework's organizing question to its largest scale, and clearly labeled as inquiry rather than established claim.

V · The Cosmic Recursion

Speculative philosophy · On Mind and Cosmos

A philosophical exploration of the parallels between physical and cognitive organization — taking seriously, and examining critically, the family of ideas that treat structure in the universe and structure in the mind as expressions of a shared grammar.

Planned · Conceptual

VI · The Mayon Paradox

Speculative philosophy · A Synthesis

The closing synthesis: an attempt to reconcile the individual and the collective, self-possession and interdependence, into a single coherent picture of a flourishing life. The capstone of the arc, and the most exploratory volume in it.

Planned · Conceptual

Research

The M·A·H·A Framework

Working paper · An Integrative Architecture for Resisting Systemic Metabolic, Attentional, and Relational Extraction

The formal foundation. Formalizes the framework as an integrative socio-biological model: that contemporary metabolic, attentional, and relational pathologies are coupled downstream consequences of a single extractive architecture. Develops the proposed couplings, sets boundary conditions, and concludes with a formal falsifiability protocol — every claim tagged by evidence level.

Ongoing

A Note on Method

This is an active research program, not a finished system. Items marked in development or planned are exactly that — work underway or intended, listed openly rather than implied.

The models offered across this work are conceptual tools, not final answers. They are meant to be examined, refined, criticized, and extended. If they prove useful, it will not be because they end inquiry, but because they help organize it. That has been the purpose from the beginning.
